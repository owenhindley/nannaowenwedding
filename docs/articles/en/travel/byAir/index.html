If you don't already live in Iceland, it's very likely you'll need to book a ticket on a plane to get to our wedding.

(because Iceland is surrounded by sea and quite a long way away from anything else.)

Keflavik is a small, cosy airport, with a very well stocked duty-free (see Food & Drinks). If you look out of the right window when you're coming in from Europe, you might just see the latest volcano smoking away.

There's also an airport in Reykjavik City, but that's only for domestic trips.

From Keflavik Airport your options are:


  • Take a transfer bus to Reykjavik
    (45 minutes - we reccomend FlyBus) Depending on when you arrive, spend some time there before taking another bus to Þórsmörk Básar (options for public buses)
    - or -
  • Hire a small car at the airport
    Go for a sightseeing drive, park the car near Seljalandsfoss and take a bus to Þórsmörk Básar (you can't drive a regular car the whole way due to river crossings)
    - or -
  • Hire a 4x4 car at the airport
    If you're experienced at 4x4 driving on rugged terrain, and are confident with deep river crossings, you can hire a bigger car and drive the whole way to Básar yourself. There's one significant river crossing, to a depth of around 1m (depending on the weather). Check with the rental company that it's suitable for F-roads, and that you can drive to Básar.
    - or -
  • Hire a 4x4 campervan
    See Accommodation section - and solve your transport and accommodation at the same time! See above notes about 4x4 rentals and the route.

We're going to try and get some discounts for you all with local hire companies - we'll keep this page updated if we do. We may also be chartering / hiring buses to make the journey more straightforward (and fun!).



Þósmörk does technically have its own airport (BITM), but you might struggle to find people to fly you there, and most importantly there is no duty-free on arrival (or buildings of any kind), so we recommend Keflavik to be on the safe side.
