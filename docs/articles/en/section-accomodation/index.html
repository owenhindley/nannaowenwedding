Being a stunningly remote location in the Highlands, there are no 5-star hotels nearby. You'll have three main options for accomodation - a bed in the cabin, a campervan, or bringing your own tent.

The Cabins

Basar has two large wooden cabins, and sleeps 75 friendly people across two floors each.

As you can see from the photos, it's bunk-bed style sleeping, and you'll need to bring your own bedding (a sleeping bag or duvet / bedcovers). The cabins are both very warm and cosy, have power points, toilets, showers (with underfloor heating!) and kitchen which you'll have full acess to regardless of whether you're staying there or not.

We're getting a special deal on the cabins, two nights for 10,000 ISK per person.

Campervans

If you're an experienced off-road driver, and comfortable with river crossings (there's several, with the largest around 1m deep and 20m wide), you can rent a 4x4 campervan from (link).

Note that if there's been heavy rain, the river might be higher than usual, and you may have to take a bus - but we'll be travelling there ahead of time, so can let everyone know what the state of the road is.

The campsite charges 2000 ISK per person per night for campervans.

Tents

Tents are the simplest option, and camping in the woods at Þórsmörk is a great experience. You can find a nice secluded spot in the forest next to a river (to keep the beer cool), or camp in one of the large, open spaces with benches and public grills. The area is large, but easy to navigate.

The campsite charges 2000 ISK per person per night for tents.
