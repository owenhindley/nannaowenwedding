The Location - Básar, Þórsmörk
. . . . . . . . . .

Þórsmörk is a valley in South Iceland -
bordered on one side by Eyjafjallajökull
(yep, that volcano from 2010)
and the Icelandic highlands on the other.

The campground / cabin area in Þórsmörk we'll be in is called Básar, the largest and best-equipped in the valley. It's also the most accessible, as you don't have to cross the main river (Krossá) to get there.

It's surrounded by dramatic rocky outcrops, cosy forests, tiny glacial streams and woody paths winding up to epic mountainside meadows where you might just catch sight of an arctic fox - or find some wild blueberries to snack on.

In one direction you can see glaciers, or the red-orange mountains towards Landmannalaugar, in the other direction you can almost see to the coast and the North Sea.

It's one of the most beautiful places in all of Iceland, and we're excited to transform this stunning, rustic place into our own little festival for you all, with soundsystem, lights and live music. (And if you want to perform too, that's great!)


The Weather

There's a saying in Iceland: "If you don't like the weather, just wait 5 minutes".

There's another saying: "There's no such thing as bad weather, just being poorly dressed".

Although Þórsmörk is famed for having more pleasant weather than its surrounding area, due to the fact that the area is circled by mountains that keep most of the wind at bay, there is still a high chance of unpleasant weather.

This may come in the form of rain, wind or even snow/hail. If we're lucky, there'll be pleasant sunshine throughout the weekend, but you'd be wise to prepare for the worst but hope for the best. Also note that the area is in a green birch area, right next to a glacier and a volcano, so contrasts can be extreme in both landscape and weather.

Temperature may range from anything between 5°C-25°C, but one thing is for certain - there will be daylight until close to midnight, and darkness will only last for about 2-3 hours.
