By Air

If you don't already live in Iceland, it's very likely you'll need to book a ticket on a plane to get from where you currently live to our wedding, on account of it being surrounded by sea, and quite a long way away from anything else.

Þósmörk does technically have its own airport (BITM - link), but you might struggle to find people to fly you there, and most importantly there is no duty-free on arrival (or actual buildings of any kind), so we recommend Keflavik International Airport (KEF) to be on the safe side.

Keflavik is a small, cosy airport, with a very well stocked duty-free (see Food & Drinks). If you look out of the right window when you're coming in from Europe, you might just see the latest volcano smoking away (if you're coming from the US, you're out of luck). There's also an airport in Reykjavik City, but that's only for domestic trips (with some exceptions).

From Keflavik Airport your options are:

  • Take a transfer bus to Reykjavik (45 minutes - we reccomend FlyBus), and depending on when you arrive, spend some time there before taking another bus to Þórsmörk Básar (options for public buses)
  • Hire a regular car at the airport (recommendations), go for a sightseeing drive, and park the car near Seljalandsfoss and take a bus to Þórsmörk Básar (you can't drive a regular car the whole way due to river crossings)
  • If you're experienced at 4x4 off-road driving, and are confident driving through rivers, you can hire a bigger car (ideally one with a periscope) and drive the whole way to Básar yourself. There's one significant river crossing, to a depth of around 1m (depending on the weather). There's also 4x4 campervans available from (link).
