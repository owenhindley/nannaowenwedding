You can travel by sea from Europe to Iceland!


The Smyril Line ferry travels from Hirtshals in Denmark to beautiful Seyðisfjörður on the East coast of Iceland, taking 2-3 days, depending on the schedule (link)

It's a car ferry, so it's ideal if you're looking to make a bigger trip with a campervan or your own car. The ferry also stops off at the Faroe Islands along the way. Once you land in Iceland, you'll then need to drive yourself to Seljalandsfoss (we'd reccomend taking at least two days to do this, there's plenty to see on the way), and then either transfer to a bus going to Þórsmörk Básar (you can't drive a regular car or van the whole way due to river crossings), or hire a 4x4 to drive the whole way if you're confident (see above).
There are buses going that way, if you want to keep things public transport all the way, from Egilsstaðir - see Straeto (Icelandic public bus system)

If you're taking the ferry away from Iceland, you can take the 'north way around' via Akureyri back to Seyðisfjörður, for which we'd reccommend spending at least 2-3 days.
