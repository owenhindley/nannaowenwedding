If you don't already live in Iceland, it's very likely you'll need to book a ticket on a plane to get to our wedding.

(because Iceland is surrounded by sea and quite a long way away from anything else.)

Keflavik is a small airport in the south-west of Iceland, and if you look out of the right window when you're coming in from mainland Europe, you can see Þórsmörk from above!

From Keflavik Airport - it's a 4 hour drive with stops to the venue (3 hours from Reykjavík).

We highly recommend arriving on the Thursday at the latest to break up the journey, spending a night in either Reykjavík, or Hvolsvöllur, and then arriving at Básar on the Friday.

We've arranged a discount for the Thursday night for a limited number of rooms at Midgard hostel in Hvolsvöllur - you can book directly with them by email, and by mentioning "Nanna & Owen's Wedding" you'll be able to get either :

  • Ensuite 4-person family room (8000isk / person)
  • Shared bathroom 4-person family room (7225isk / person)
  • Bed in a 6-person dorm (7500isk / person)

Once you've landed, your transport options are:


  • Take a transfer bus to Reykjavik, and another bus to Básar
    (45 minutes - we recommend FlyBus) Depending on when you arrive, spend some time in Reykjavik before taking another bus to Þórsmörk Básar.
    - or -
  • Hire a small car at the airport, drive to Hvolsvöllur, take a bus
    Go for a sightseeing drive, park the car at Hvolsvöllur and take a bus to Þórsmörk Básar (you can't drive a regular car the whole way due to river crossings)
    - or -
  • Hire a 4x4 at the airport, drive the whole way
    If you're experienced at 4x4 driving on rugged terrain, and are confident with deep river crossings, you can hire a bigger car and drive the whole way to Básar yourself. There's one significant river crossing, to a depth of around 1m (depending on the weather). Check with the rental company that it's suitable for F-roads, and that you can drive to Básar.
    - or -
  • Hire a 4x4 campervan
    See Accommodation section - and solve your transport and accommodation at the same time! See above notes about 4x4 rentals and the route.

Getting back

Buses leave Básar in the afternoon, so if you're leaving Iceland on the Sunday, book your flights from Keflavik no earlier than 9pm to leave enough time for the return bus trip.


We may also be chartering / hiring buses to make the journey more straightforward (and fun!).

We realise that we're bringing you all to a place that's quite a trek to get to, but it's all part of a plan to trap you all for three days so we can hang out properly!



Þórsmörk does technically have its own airport (BITM), but you might struggle to find people to fly you there, and most importantly there is no duty-free, passport control, baggage handling staff or buildings, so we recommend Keflavik to be on the safe side.
