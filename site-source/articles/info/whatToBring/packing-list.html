Here's a suggested packing list of things to bring to make sure you're prepared for whatever the weather's throwing at us!

General:
  • Good shoes (i.e. ones that will keep your feet dry if it rains)
  • Warm thick socks
  • Thermal leggings / long johns (you can wear them underneath trousers / a skirt, and be warm whilst still looking fabulous)
  • Thermal top (see above)
  • Layers of clothes that you can put on or take off as it gets warmer / colder throughout the day.
  • Sunglasses
  • Waterproof jacket (also good if there's a bit of wind)
  • Swimsuit (if you're staying longer, we strongly reccommend taking a dip in any of the local swimming pools - they all have high quality hot tubs and saunas, and are very affordable)
If you're staying in a tent:
  • A tent
  • Good inflatable mattress / rollmat
  • Pillow (or a jacket stuffed into a T-shirt works well)
  • Sleeping bag that's comfortable down to 0 degrees (it won't get that cold, but if you bring a good one you're guaranteed a good night's sleep)
  • Eyemask (because it gets bright at 5am)
If you're staying in the cabin:
  • Sleeping bag / duvet + bedsheet (the beds are just a bare mattress, so you'll need something to cover them)
  • Pillow
  • Earplugs (in case someone's snoring)
  • Eyemask (because it gets bright at 5am)
Food & Drink
  • We're providing wine, beer and some basic spirits the whole weekend. But if there's a specific tipple you're craving, your best (and cheapest) bet is to buy it in the duty-free on your way in (alcohol tax is quite high in Iceland, especially for high-percentage drinks)
